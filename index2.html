HIYA. MY NAME IS TIA PICKENS. I’M A GRAPHIC AND UX/UI DESIGNER.
Hi! My name is Tia Pickens and I am a Graphic and UX/UI Designer. I specialize in creating user-centered experiences that are intuitive, visually appealing and functional, while also solving user problems and translating them into design solutions that enhance usability and satisfaction.
When it comes to my design thinking and thought process, I start my foundation from using the Double Diamond method, an iterative process in which I often revisit phases of my design journey as I gather more insight and then continuously refine my understanding of the problem until I can deliver a solid final product.
I love seeing the excitement and gratification from users that interact with my craft; "Do what you love, and you'll never work another day in your life" truly does apply to me. I’m a  master of many hats, and work best in any kind of environment that will allow me to use all of my skills to the fullest!

RFECY WEBSITE REDESIGN: Resources for Exceptional Children and Youth

The website "Resources for Exceptional Children and Youth" is an indispensable reserve of knowledge for Parents and Education Professionals alike, who need a place to access key information and programs for special needs children/youth. Users felt a sense of overwhelm and a general lack of guidance on finding those resources, which my team and I felt we could fix with a redesign of their UI.

ORIGINAL APP DESIGN - BABYTHRIFT Clothing App

New parents have always struggled with the financial balance between clothing and other necessities for their newborns. Enter the solution: BabyThrift, an app designed for young parents to shop for newborn and maternity clothing at affordable prices and help with sustainability and stopping unnecessary waste.

GOVERNMENT REDESIGN: US Department of Education

The US Department of Education's website is a necessary tool for higher education, in order to get further insight into financial support opportunities for post-secondary students. There was a general sense of confusion with the organization of content, navigation bars, and saturation of content and links with their users. My team's objective was then to optimize the website's navigation design, visual aesthetics, and overall usability to provide users with an enhanced user experience.
